Tokyo, Japan The historic Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa. It’s Tokyo’s oldest temple, dating back to 645 AD, and is dedicated to Kannon, the Buddhist goddess of mercy between March and April and September and November. Autumn ushers in colorful foliage and comfortable temperatures. Spring brings in much of the same, but instead of vibrant fall hues, the foliage you'll see here are cherry blossom trees in full bloom. A 7-day trip for one person on a moderate budget could cost approximately CAD 3,000-4,000, depending on airfare, accommodations, transportation,food preferences and the season​. An interesting fact about Tokyo is that it has the world’s busiest train station—Shinjuku Station. This massive station serves more than 3.5 million passengers daily. Despite its scale, the station is renowned for its punctuality and smooth operation.

Matamata, New Zealand Home of the shire, Matamata is a must see if you're a Lord of the Rings and Hobbit fan. Take a guided tour of the Hobbiton Movie Set™ and enjoy the town's fabulous cafes. The best time to visit Matamata, New Zealand is during the spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May). The weather is mild during these seasons, and the landscape is beautiful with spring flowers or autumnal colors. This makes it ideal for exploring the hobbit houses and the countryside. The total estimated cost is approximately $3,000–$4,500 CAD per person for a one-week trip, with the final price depending on accommodation type, transportation choices, and additional activities. Matamata is known for its farming and thoroughbred industry, and its highlight is the Hobbiton Movie Set. Beyond Hobbiton, there are beautiful natural sights in the region (e.g., the nearby Waitomo Caves)
